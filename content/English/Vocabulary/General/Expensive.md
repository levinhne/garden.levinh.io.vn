# Expensive /ɪkˈspensɪv/

**Part of Speech:** Adjective  
**Meaning:** Đắt tiền, đắt đỏ, tốn kém

## 📖 Definition

Something that costs a lot of money; high-priced.

## 🔤 Word Forms

| Form | Word | Example |
|------|------|---------|
| Adjective | expensive | This car is very expensive. |
| Adverb | expensively | She dresses expensively. |
| Noun | expense | The expense of living in the city is high. |
| Noun | expensiveness | The expensiveness of the restaurant surprised me. |

## 💡 Usage Examples

### Basic Usage
```
1. It's a very expensive car.
   (Đó là một chiếc xe rất đắt tiền.)

2. This restaurant is too expensive for me.
   (Nhà hàng này quá đắt đối với tôi.)

3. Diamonds are expensive jewelry.
   (Kim cương là đồ trang sức đắt tiền.)
```

### In Context
```
4. I'll take you to an expensive restaurant.
   (Tôi sẽ đưa bạn đến một nhà hàng đắt tiền.)

5. Living in New York is expensive.
   (Sống ở New York rất tốn kém.)

6. He bought an expensive gift for his wife.
   (Anh ấy mua một món quà đắt tiền cho vợ.)
```

## 🔄 Synonyms (Từ đồng nghĩa)

| Word | Pronunciation | Usage |
|------|---------------|-------|
| **costly** | /ˈkɔːstli/ | More formal, emphasizes high cost |
| **pricey** | /ˈpraɪsi/ | Informal, conversational |
| **dear** | /dɪr/ | British English, somewhat old-fashioned |
| **high-priced** | /haɪ praɪst/ | Emphasizes the high price tag |
| **overpriced** | /ˌoʊvərˈpraɪst/ | Too expensive for its value |
| **steep** | /stiːp/ | Informal, unexpectedly expensive |

### Examples with Synonyms
```
- This watch is very costly. (formal)
- That hotel is pretty pricey. (informal)
- These shoes are overpriced! (negative connotation)
- $50 for a burger? That's steep! (surprising)
```

## ⚖️ Antonyms (Từ trái nghĩa)

| Word | Pronunciation | Meaning |
|------|---------------|---------|
| **cheap** | /tʃiːp/ | Rẻ, không đắt |
| **inexpensive** | /ˌɪnɪkˈspensɪv/ | Không đắt (neutral tone) |
| **affordable** | /əˈfɔːrdəbl/ | Có thể mua được, giá phải chăng |
| **economical** | /ˌiːkəˈnɑːmɪkl/ | Tiết kiệm, không tốn kém |
| **reasonable** | /ˈriːznəbl/ | Hợp lý, phải chăng |

### Examples with Antonyms
```
- This car is cheap but reliable.
- We found an inexpensive hotel near the beach.
- The prices here are very affordable.
- This is a reasonable price for a laptop.
```

## 🗣️ Common Phrases & Idioms

### 1. "Too expensive for my blood"
**Meaning:** Quá đắt đối với tôi  
**Example:** "That sports car is too expensive for my blood."

### 2. "Expensive taste"
**Meaning:** Có gu thích đồ xa xỉ, đắt tiền  
**Example:** "She has expensive taste in fashion."

### 3. "It doesn't come cheap"
**Meaning:** Nó không rẻ đâu  
**Example:** "Quality doesn't come cheap."

### 4. "Pay through the nose"
**Meaning:** Trả giá cắt cổ  
**Example:** "I paid through the nose for this apartment."

### 5. "Cost an arm and a leg"
**Meaning:** Đắt như tôm tươi  
**Example:** "This repair will cost me an arm and a leg."

## ✍️ Writing Practice

### Sentence Patterns

1. **It's (too/very/quite) expensive**
   - It's too expensive for students.
   - It's very expensive but worth it.

2. **[Noun] is expensive**
   - Living in this city is expensive.
   - Healthcare is expensive in the US.

3. **An expensive [noun]**
   - He bought an expensive watch.
   - She stays at expensive hotels.

4. **More expensive than...**
   - Gold is more expensive than silver.
   - This brand is more expensive than others.

5. **The most expensive [noun]**
   - This is the most expensive item in the store.
   - What's the most expensive thing you've bought?

## 🌍 Cultural Notes

### Price Sensitivity by Country
- **US/UK:** People often say "pricey" or "steep" informally
- **Common phrases:** "That's a bit much" / "That's quite dear" (UK)
- **Tipping culture:** Even expensive restaurants expect 15-20% tips in the US

### Shopping Context
- In many English-speaking countries, it's acceptable to say something is "too expensive" when shopping
- Negotiating prices is common in markets but not in regular stores
- Saying "Do you have anything cheaper?" is polite and normal
